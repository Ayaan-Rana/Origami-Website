Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil.
  • Their predators, or animals that will eat them, don’t know they’re coming.

Chameleon

  • Chameleons actually change their color due to a number of different factors, including their mood, changes in light or temperature, or the humidity of their environment.
  • Chameleons have eyes in the back of their heads.
  • Chameleons’ tongues are extremely quick and powerful, sometimes exceeding accelerations of 41g.

Camel

  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  • When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  • Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.

Teddy Bear

  • Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals.
  • The bear is a common national personification for Russia (and the former USSR) and Germany.
  • Vikings and the Celts have many legends about the strength, protectiveness and prowess of bears.

Panda

  • They have an extended wrist bone that they use like a thumb to help them grip food.
  • Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.
  • Pandas need at least 2 different bamboo species in their range to avoid starvation.
